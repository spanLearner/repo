Directive principles of state policy

The DPSP are mentionend in the Part IV of the indian constitution from articles 36 to 51

The idea of DPSP is borrowed from the irish constitution

Article 37 deals with application of the principles contained in this part

The directive principles are non - justiciable.

The constitution (article 37) itself says that these principles are fundamental in the governance of the country and it shall be the duty of the state to apply these principles in making laws.

They embody the concept of welfare state

Article 38 says that the state to secure a social order for the promotion of the welfare of the people

Article 39A added by 42nd amendment provides for the the equal justice and free legal aid
