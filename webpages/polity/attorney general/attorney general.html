Attorney general of india

The indian Constitution Provides for the office of teh attorney General of india

he is the Highest Law officer in teh country

The President shall appoint a person who is qualified to be appointed a judge of the supreme court to be the attorney general for India Article 76

It shall be the duty of attorney general of india to give advice to the govenment of India upon such legal matters and to perform such other duties of a legal character, as amy from time to time be referred or assigned to him by the president Article 76(2)

In the performance of his duties the attorney general shall have Right of audience in all courts in teh territory of india. Article 76(3)

Attorney general shall hold the office during the pleasure of the president and shall receive such remuneration as teh president may determine in the conduct of government business Article 76(4)

He has the right to speak and to take part in the prooceedings of both the houses of the parliament or their joint sitting but without a right to vote

He enjoys all the privileges and immunities that are available to a member of parliament

Comptroller and auditor general of india

Article 148(1) CAG shall be appointed by teh president and shall only be removed from office in manner or on the grounds as a judge of the suprement court.

The CAG shall not be eligible for further office either under the govenment of india or any state after he has ceased to hold his office Article 148(4)

He holds office for a period of 6 years or up to the age of 65 years.

He can be removed by the president on the basis of a resolution passed to that effect by both the houses of Parliament with special majority, either on the ground of proved Misbehaviour or incapacity

He is the guardian of Public purse and controls the entire financial system of the country at both the levels- the centre and the state

He is called the Eye and Ear of the public accounts committee

He submits his audit reports relating to the accounts of a state to governor who shall in turn place them before the state legislature Article 151(2) article 151(2) to the president and he submits it to the parliament
